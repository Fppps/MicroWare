At a glance

Now shipping

MicWin 1

  • Foundation model for labs and learning.
  • Ships with curated medical information to enable offline testing, red‑teaming, and UI prototyping.
  • Not recommended for diagnosis, treatment, or clinical workflows.
  • Includes a PS6 single‑use profile for console demos and kiosk trials.
  • Known quirk: occasional playful behavior if you press Regenerate; more tame than 1.5.
Best for: classrooms, hackdays, internal sandboxes.
Enhanced

MicWin 1.5

  • Developer‑ready with a sense of humor.
  • Programming language support for common stacks (JS/TS, Python, HTML/CSS, and more) to help scaffold, explain, and refactor code.
  • Expanded health knowledge for testing health‑related UIs and flows (still not recommended for real‑world medical use).
  • Known quirk: if the user presses Regenerate, responses can get a bit weird—by design, for stress‑testing UX.
Best for: creators and developers who want language‑aware assistance without extra complexity.
Next

MicWin 2‑lite

  • Focused domains. Lean runtime.
  • Video testing utilities for trying prompt‑to‑script and narration experiments.
  • Astrophysics sandbox for conceptual Q&A and unit conversions.
  • Health questions preview mode for UI prototyping (still not for medical use).
  • Social meta helpers for caption/draft ideation and trend summaries.
Releasing 8/28/25 @ 7:00 PM EST • Ideal for: production‑adjacent demos and lean web apps.
